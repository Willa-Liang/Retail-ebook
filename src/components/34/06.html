Meetings Should Create Movement

Cadence

RitualsExecution

A recurring meeting should either resolve a decision, remove a blocker, or align owners around a next step. If it does none of these, it is usually reporting disguised as collaboration.

Replacing broad updates with short exception-based reviews gives teams time back while preserving the information leaders actually need.

Example: circulate updates asynchronously and use the meeting only for issues that need intervention.

This turns attendance into active problem-solving rather than passive listening.

The best meeting is often the one that leaves with fewer open loops than it started with.

Translation:

A meeting earns its place when it compresses uncertainty and accelerates action.
