Decision Speed Is Designed

Governance

ApprovalFlow

Slow decisions are often framed as cultural problems, but many are structural. Too many reviewers, unclear thresholds, and missing default rules create hesitation.

A better model defines what can be decided locally, what requires escalation, and what is approved automatically when standard conditions are met.

Example: pre-approve common exceptions under a value threshold.

Teams can then reserve senior review for truly high-risk choices.

Good governance does not mean touching every decision. It means shaping the few points where intervention changes the outcome.

Translation:

Escalation should be a precision tool, not the default operating mode.
