StoresInsight

Store and service teams see friction first. They know where customers wait, where policies fail, and where a workaround becomes the real process.

When leadership creates a simple loop for submitting and testing ideas, operational improvements move from opinion to evidence within days instead of quarters.

Example: collect top three repeated blockers every Friday and test one fix each Monday.

A small cadence beats a large innovation program that never reaches execution.

The people closest to the work usually know what is slowing it down.

Translation:

Innovation is not a department-only activity. It becomes sustainable when operators can contribute safely and consistently.
