Reduce Waste, Not Capacity

Allocation

CostResourcing

Across volatile periods, blanket cuts appear decisive but often remove the same percentage from high-value work and low-value work alike.

A stronger response is selective allocation: remove duplicated reporting, approval bottlenecks, and low-signal meetings while protecting customer impact and strategic build capacity.

Example: remove one weekly review and reinvest that hour into store-level problem solving.

The gain is not only saved time; it is faster local decision-making.

Healthy organizations keep reserve energy. What matters is whether that reserve protects resilience or hides friction.

Translation:

Slack can be strategic. Waste is work that produces no learning, no customer value, and no leverage.
