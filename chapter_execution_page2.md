# OPERATIONS

For retailers, store results depend on how HQ and stores can execute together. Cross‑function coordination matters.

> Operations teams spend less time reconstructing store activity and more time completing work still in motion.

**Operations breaks down when HQ sees store reality too late.**

Store activities can be misreported or update sporadically while operational gaps remain unresolved. Rollout programs can run misaligned from the field. Operations leaders must chase emails, chats, calls, and spreadsheets across systems, before issues reach the right owners.

As networks expand, the execution gap between HQ and stores can widen:
- readiness is verified before opening
- rollout activity remains visible centrally
- exceptions escalate immediately
- operational signals translate across teams
